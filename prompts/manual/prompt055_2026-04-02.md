- Root-caused this specific test: GIL contention prevents the poll thread from reading get_current_state() while CPU-intensive PyTorch candidate training runs

Root cause analysis:

- The test starts DemoMode training (demo.start()), then polls demo.get_current_state()["hidden_units"] every 0.5s for 90 seconds
- get_current_state() acquires self._lock — the lock itself is only held briefly by the training thread
- However, train_candidate_pool() runs 32 candidates × 600 steps of tight PyTorch tensor ops. On Python 3.14, the poll thread gets starved and never reads the state, even though cascade units ARE being installed (confirmed by logs showing units at +52s)
- After the 90s deadline expires, demo.stop() is called, but by then found_units is still 0

Remaining work:

- Rewrite the test to wait for training completion instead of polling during training. The training will naturally complete (max_epochs=300, max_hidden_units=3). Wait for demo.is_running to become False (no lock needed — boolean read is atomic in CPython), then assert on the final state
- Alternative approach: use a threading.Event set by the training loop on cascade install, and event.wait(timeout=90) in the test
